Superstore Sales — Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

A beginner-friendly data analysis project exploring sales, profit, and discount patterns in a fictional retail superstore dataset.

Objective
Understand what drives sales and profitability across categories, regions, and customer segments using Python-based EDA.

Key Questions Answered
- Which product category generates the most revenue?
- Which region is most profitable?
- Does offering higher discounts hurt profit?
- What does the monthly sales trend look like?
- How are customers distributed across segments?

Key Findings
- **Technology** leads in total sales; **Office Supplies** has the highest order volume
- **Discounts negatively correlate with profit** — higher discounts often result in losses
- **West** and **East** regions outperform Central and South
- **Consumers** make up ~52% of all orders

Tech Stack
| Tool | Purpose |

Output Preview
The script generates 6 charts:
1. Total Sales by Category (bar)
2. Total Profit by Region (bar)
3. Discount vs Profit (scatter + trendline)
4. Sales Distribution (histogram)
5. Orders by Customer Segment (pie)
6. Monthly Sales Trend (line)
